## Introduction

We know how challenging it is to raise a child with attention and focus difficulties. We've prepared a guide explaining the professional approach behind BUFF — a system built in collaboration with experts, based on evidence-based Executive Functioning principles.

This document is the **single source of truth** for BUFF's philosophy. It informs all:
- UI copy and strings
- AI prompts (for task/reward generation)
- Feature design decisions
- Marketing and onboarding

## The 6 Pillars

The six conceptual foundations of the product. Each is a reframing of a classic parenting challenge for an ADHD child.

### 1. ⚡ The Name "Buff" — Empowerment

In gaming, "Buff" is a power-up bonus. We turn daily routines into adventures using the language kids love — quests, points, and a reward shop.

> 💡 **Insight:** Gaming language minimizes resistance and increases engagement in children with attention difficulties.

---

### 2. 🏆 Dopamine Bridge — Smart Rewards

Distant rewards don't motivate an ADHD brain. We built a tiered system — from small daily rewards to a fun family day after 10 success days.

> 💡 **Insight:** Small, frequent rewards are more effective than one large distant reward.

---

### 3. 🛡️ Safe Harbor — Positive Only

Your child can only earn points — **never lose them**. No deductions, no punishments. An incomplete task simply didn't earn points.

> 💡 **Insight:** Positive reinforcement builds self-confidence and long-term intrinsic motivation.

---

### 4. ❤️ Parent Bonus — The Invisible Effort

Staying focused, restraining impulses — enormous effort that goes unrecorded. The "Daily Win Bonus" is your way to say: _"I see you today."_

> 💡 **Insight:** The bonus serves as an emotional bridge and strengthens the child's sense of agency.

---

### 5. 📅 Day Types — Energy Management

Kids with ADHD "run a marathon while others sprint." The system auto-adjusts tasks for school days vs. rest days.

> 💡 **Insight:** Adjusting expectations reduces cognitive load and prevents burnout.

---

### 6. 🔥 The 70% Goal — Winning Streak

We don't chase perfection. Reaching 70% is the real success that builds confidence and a winning streak.

> 💡 **Insight:** Realistic goals are the key to preventing dropout.

---

## 4 Core Principles

Practical principles that translate into features and behaviors.

### ⏱️ The 15-Minute Rule
Encourage starting with a tiny task for just 15 minutes — to generate dopamine and overcome initiation difficulty.

### 🤝 Partnership, Not Policing
The app manages the routine — the parent shifts from enforcer to coach who asks: _"What does BUFF show for today?"_

### 😴 Rest Is Part of the Training
Rest Tickets teach that proactive rest is part of long-term success, not surrender.

### 🧩 Planner vs. Executor
Parent plans, child executes. This reduces cognitive load and decision paralysis for the child.

## How to Use This Document

### For UI Copy
Use the fixed terminology. Don't write "tasks" — write "Missions." Don't write "points" — write "Buffs."

### For AI Prompts
When asking Claude/GPT to generate missions or rewards, include the principles (positive reinforcement only, break into parts, frequent small rewards).

### For Feature Design
Check that every feature aligns with the philosophy. A feature that allows "deducting points" contradicts Safe Harbor — **don't build it**.

### For Marketing & Onboarding
Every marketing/onboarding message should echo one principle from the philosophy — not just "what we do" but "why it works."
